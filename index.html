I was never particularly good at maths before year 11 of my highschool. I wasn't bad either, I was just mediocre. I memorized the formulas and did what my teacher told me in certain questions. It was as if they wanted me to use just a different cookie-cutter answer for a particular question. I didn't understand much intuitively, that is before I met an extremely passionate teacher that was actually interested in making us think creatively about the given problem. That teacher's perspective on brought about a revolution in how I think and that I found maths genuinely interesting. Her method was as follows; Spend a lot of time laying the foundations by starting from scratch and not assume that everybody knows the basics. Then start by working through a typical problem in the given topic with everybody and then start introducing irregularities in said problems to promote more involve critical thinking to come up with a creative solution. Every one of those problems was unique and required a unique approach. The best way to teach people is to first lay down the fundamental knowledge and then guide them through the curveball questions so that next time they would be able to come up with a solution themselves.
